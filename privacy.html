Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 14, 2026
Effective Date: April 14, 2026

1. Professional Scope

This Privacy Policy governs the processing of data by DFUCapp. This App is a professional clinical decision support tool designed exclusively for licensed healthcare professionals. It is not intended for patient self-diagnosis or use by the general public.

2. Data Collection

2.1 Clinician Information

We collect identity and contact data (Name, Email, Institution) to maintain secure access and clinical audit trails. Biometric data used for login (FaceID/TouchID) is processed exclusively via the iOS Secure Enclave and is never transmitted to our servers.

2.2 Patient Clinical Data

Clinicians enter Protected Health Information (PHI) regarding patients under their care, including demographics, clinical findings, and photographs of foot ulcers. This data is collected solely for clinical documentation and risk assessment.

3. AI-Assisted Analysis

DFUCapp utilizes Vision Transformer (ViT) models to assist in classifying diabetic foot ulcers. Image processing for AI analysis is performed securely. These images are not used for marketing or unauthorized secondary purposes.

Medical Disclaimer

DFUCapp does not provide medical diagnoses. AI-generated risk levels are suggestions only. The attending clinician remains the final authority on all clinical decisions and management plans.

4. Storage and Security

Data is stored using Supabase infrastructure on SOC2-compliant servers located in Singapore, adhering to PDPA and international security standards. We employ AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 encryption in transit.

The App includes a privacy overlay that automatically masks clinical data in the iOS App Switcher to prevent accidental exposure of patient information.

5. Account Deletion

In accordance with Apple App Store guidelines, users may initiate account deletion at any time via Settings > Account > Delete Account within the App. Upon request, clinician profile data will be permanently purged from our active databases.

6. Clinician Responsibilities

As the primary Data Controller, the Clinician is responsible for:

  • Obtaining necessary patient consent for clinical photography and data storage.
  • Ensuring use of the App complies with local institutional privacy policies.
  • Protecting any data exported from the App as a PDF or report.

7. Contact & Rights

Under Vietnam's PDPD and Singapore's PDPA, you have the right to access, correct, or request the deletion of your personal data. For all inquiries, please contact our Data Protection Officer:
